# Bevy Ultrasonic Raycasting Implementation Guide

Here is the comprehensive blueprint for coding your analytical ultrasonic
simulation in Bevy.

## 1. Engine Setup and Component Structure

- **Physics Plugin:** Integrate a physics backend (`avian` or `bevy_rapier`)
  strictly for its spatial query API. Disable internal gravity and collision
  resolution if they interfere with your target movement.
- **Components Needed:**
  - `UltrasonicSensor`: Stores $f_t$ (base frequency), $c$ (speed of sound),
    $\sigma$ (pulse width), and ray configuration parameters (beam angle, ray
    count).
  - `HitHistory`: Stores the previous frame's hit distances for calculating
    velocity.
  - `Reflector`: A marker component to distinguish targets from static clutter,
    though velocity calculation natively handles this.

## 2. The Raycast Loop (Data Collection)

- **Cone Casting:** In your update system, generate a bundle of rays originating
  from the sensor's `Transform`, spread across your desired beam angle.
- **Execution:** Fire these rays every frame or at a fixed time step.
- **Classification:** For every hit, record the entity, the hit point, and the
  distance ($d_{current}$).

## 3. Per-Hit Calculations (Physics & Math)

For each valid ray hit, calculate the required parameters independently.

- **Relative Velocity ($v$):** Retrieve the previous distance ($d_{previous}$)
  for that specific hit point/entity from your `HitHistory`.
  $$v = \frac{d_{current} - d_{previous}}{\Delta t}$$ _(Note: Static floor
  clutter will naturally yield $v = 0$)._
- **Time of Flight ($t_{delay}$):** $$t_{delay} = \frac{2d_{current}}{c}$$
- **Two-Way Doppler Shift ($f_r$):**
  $$f_r = f_t \left( \frac{c - v}{c + v} \right)$$ _(Note: For static clutter,
  $f_r$ equals $f_t$)._

## 4. Signal Synthesis (The Carrier Wave)

Do not sum the envelopes. You must calculate the high-frequency wave for each
hit to simulate realistic acoustic interference.

- **Envelope Function ($E(t)$):** Compute the amplitude at a given time $t$
  using a Gaussian pulse:
  $$E_i(t) = e^{-\frac{(t - t_{delay,i})^2}{2\sigma^2}}$$
- **Individual Waveform ($S_i(t)$):** Combine the envelope and the shifted
  carrier frequency:
  $$S_i(t) = E_i(t) \cdot \cos(2\pi f_{r,i} (t - t_{delay,i}))$$

## 5. Superposition and Final Processing

- **Aggregation:** Sum all individual waveforms together into a single global
  signal array at each time step $t$. This handles both target multi-path
  reflections and floor clutter simultaneously.
  $$S_{total}(t) = \sum_{i=1}^{N} S_i(t)$$
- **Envelope Extraction:** To get the final shape of $S_{total}(t)$ for
  evaluation, implement an envelope detection algorithm (e.g., squaring the
  signal and applying a low-pass filter, or using a Hilbert transform
  approximation).
- **Clutter Mitigation:** To identify the moving target within the resulting
  envelope, implement a baseline subtraction or set an amplitude threshold to
  filter out the high-amplitude, stationary peaks generated by the floor.
